What is Epigenetics?

Nearly all cells in your body have the exact same DNA, yet a brain cell and a skin cell look and act completely different. Why? Because they don’t use the same parts of the DNA manual.

🎛️
The Control System
Epigenetics is the system that controls which genes are turned ON or OFF in different cells, without rewriting the actual A-T-C-G genetic code.
🌪️
Environmental Drift
Diet, stress, and toxins subtly change how genes are used. This is why identical twins develop biological differences over time.
Key Mechanisms: DNA methylation, histone modifications, and chromatin remodeling are the physical ways the body achieves this regulation.

What are Methylomes?

Sometimes, a tiny chemical "tag" called a methyl group sticks to the letter C (Cytosine) in your DNA. A Methylome is the complete map of every single tag in a cell's DNA.

Methylated (Tagged)

Genes are MOSTLY switched OFF. The physical tag blocks the machinery that reads the DNA.

Unmethylated (Naked)

Genes are MOSTLY switched ON. The DNA is accessible and active.

CpG Sites & Disease

A CpG site is a specific location where a Cytosine (C) is directly followed by a Guanine (G). These are the primary regulatory checkpoints in the genome.

In a normal cell, only specific Cytosines are methylated. In a cancer cell, the methylation pattern goes haywire in two main ways:
Hypermethylation
Too many methyl tags. Important genes (like tumor suppressors) get permanently shut OFF.
vs
Hypomethylation
Too few methyl tags. Genes that should stay OFF become actively dangerous.

Regions where this happens consistently are called Differentially Methylated Regions (DMRs).

Epigenetic Clocks

As you get older, your methylation patterns change in a highly predictable way. Machine learning models, called Epigenetic Clocks, look at these patterns to predict your biological age.

Age 20:   C  C  [CH3]  C  C       ← some sites methylated
Age 40:   C  C  [CH3]  C  [CH3]   ← more sites methylated
Age 60: [CH3] C [CH3]  C  [CH3]   ← even more sites methylated
⏱️
1st Gen Clocks
Trained strictly on chronological age. (e.g., Horvath, Hannum). They answer: "How old are you?"
❤️‍🩹
2nd Gen Clocks
Trained on blood biomarkers & mortality data. (e.g., GrimAge, PhenoAge). They answer: "What is your risk of death?"

The "Invisible" Problem

How do we actually read these tags? DNA sequencing machines are "blind" to methylation. If a machine reads a DNA strand, it just sees A, C, G, and T. It cannot see the tiny chemical tags.

Strand A (Normal)

G-A-C-T

Strand B (Methylated)

G-A-C*-T
If we sequence them normally, the computer says they are both just GACT. We completely lose the methylation information!

The Bisulfite Treatment Trick

Before sequencing, we soak the DNA in Sodium Bisulfite. This chemical is highly aggressive toward unprotected C's.

By comparing the treated sequence to a reference genome, we can clearly see which C's were protected (methylated).

QC Result: The "Expected Fail"

When running FastQC on our data, the "Per base sequence content" failed immediately. This is a massive success.

Why failing is passing

In standard DNA, A ≈ T and C ≈ G. But remember the Bisulfite Trick! Almost every unmethylated 'C' in the sample was chemically converted into a 'T'.

  • The T line (red) shoots up to the top.
  • The C line (blue) drops to nearly zero.

The software thinks the machine is broken, but we know the chemical conversion worked perfectly.

Results: Clock Correlation

We generated correlation matrices to see how strongly the different clocks agreed with one another.

  • The Consensus: Horvathv1, Hannum, Lin, and PhenoAge form a tight group with correlations > 0.87. They rank people similarly.
  • The Outlier (DunedinPACE): Correlations were low (0.22 - 0.43). This is expected! DunedinPACE predicts the pace of aging (a speedometer), not chronological age (an odometer).
  • The Failure (Zhang_10): Shows weak agreement. It only uses 10 CpG sites, proving that minimal information equals weaker reliability.
Sample Size Matters: In GSE41169 (95 samples), all correlations were higher and more consistent than in GSE120307 (34 samples).

Results: Mean Absolute Error (MAE)

MAE tells us how many years OFF the clock's prediction is compared to chronological age. Lower is better.

Horvathv1 is the most accurate (~3-4 years off). Zhang_10 has catastrophic error (~38 years off) because it was designed for mortality risk, not absolute age estimation.

Summary & Takeaways

🏆
The Gold Standard
Horvathv1, Hannum, and PhenoAge are the most reliable clocks for age prediction, tracking chronological age tightly with low error.
⚠️
Epigenetic Age Acceleration
If predicted age > real age, you have positive EAA. Your cells are aging faster, which is linked to higher disease risk.
